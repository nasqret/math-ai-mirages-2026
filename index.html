Bartosz Naskręcki Adam Mickiewicz University Centre for Credible AI, WUT
Open with the conclusion: the capability jump is real. The strongest public narrative still confuses a correct answer, a checked proof, a new theorem, and a new theory. This talk separates those achievements.
Verdict

The capability jump is real.
The popular theory of it is not.

tool-using research loops work proof assistants close real gaps autonomy remains conditional
This is neither dismissal nor victory lap. Long tool-using runs, formal proof search, and durable artifacts are real advances. But one benchmark does not describe a profession, fluent prose is not proof, and an isolated success is not general autonomy.

Trust boundary

Lean certifies less—and more—than people think.

CERTIFIES
  • the formal statement
  • the proof term
  • declared dependencies
DOES NOT CERTIFY
  • the intended theorem
  • natural assumptions
  • novelty or importance

Trust the kernel. Audit the statement.

Formal verification narrows the error surface; it does not erase mathematical judgement. A machine can correctly prove the wrong formalization. Humans still own statement alignment, explanatory value, novelty, and credit.
