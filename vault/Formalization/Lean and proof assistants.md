# Lean and proof assistants

Formal proof provides a machine-checkable endpoint, but the statement,
definitions, domains, exception predicates, axioms, and imports remain human
choices. The three paper projects expose different interfaces:

- [[Zsigmondy Chebyshev]]: an existence theorem equivalent to eight exception
  families;
- [[FinEqs]]: finite-linear-algebra compression with exact zero-set equality;
- [[EML factory]]: partial semantics and an evaluation-preserving compiler.

The kernel certifies a term against a formal statement. It does not compare that
statement with the paper. The concrete review questions are exception-list
fidelity, equivalence of auxiliary lemmas, and domain/grammar fidelity.

See [[EML factory]] and [[Aristotle and autoformalization]].
